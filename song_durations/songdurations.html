I: Introduction

There is no doubting the effect that the app TikTok has had on the way that media is produced and consumed since its launch in the U.S. in 2018. Short-form content has made its way into almost every social media app and has changed the way many people receive entertainment. There has been talk online that modern popular music artists are catering towards being easily formatted into short-form content, which has had the effect of shortening songs in general.

I decided to test this out.

I hypothesize that the most popular songs (Billboard Top 100) in the U.S. have gotten shorter since the launch of TikTok in 2018. The relevant population data would be any song that had charted within the year on the Billboard Hot 100 in the U.S., which is based on sales (physical and digital), online streaming, and radio airplay in the U.S. This gives us an idea of what the most popular songs for an entire year in the United States. I decided to choose the past 20 years, since 2004.

II: Data Description

Cleaning

My data comes from a dataset on the data science platform Kaggle called Billboard Hot-100 [2000-2023] data with features. The dataset did include durations for each song, however there were lots missing. Using Python and the Spotify API I got the song durations myself. The data cleaning included extracting the song name, artist, chart year, and duration (in seconds).

Final Sample

I decided to include duplicate songs across years because I am focused on what types of songs and durations people are listening to in any given year. The final sample was just the top 100 Year Single from the entire year, because I don't have the Spotify API credits to collect the data for any song that had charted on the Hot 100 during the entire year.

Therefore, we are missing some popular songs, but we get a good idea of what the popular conscience in the U.S. was during each year. Out of the 2400 expected songs and duration, Spotify failed to find durations for 16 songs. Therefore, the final sample has 2,384 songs and their durations since 2000.
